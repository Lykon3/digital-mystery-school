Seal of Solomon

Surface Layer - Historical

The hexagram, composed of two interlaced triangles, has been used across cultures as a symbol of divine protection and cosmic harmony. Known as the Star of David in Jewish tradition and the Seal of Solomon in Islamic lore.

Psychological Layer - Archetypal

Represents the integration of opposites - masculine and feminine, conscious and unconscious, active and receptive. Jung saw it as a mandala of psychological wholeness, the reconciliation of conflicting aspects of the psyche.

Esoteric Layer - Mystical

The upward triangle represents fire, aspiration, and divine ascent. The downward triangle represents water, receptivity, and divine descent. Their union creates the perfect balance necessary for magical operation and spiritual realization.

Personal Layer - Contemplative

Use this symbol for meditation on balance and integration. It helps harmonize opposing forces within yourself and creates a protective field of equilibrium around your spiritual practice.

Scales of Justice

Surface Layer - Historical

Ancient symbol of justice and moral balance, held by Themis and later Justice personified. Used in Egyptian mythology where Anubis weighs the heart against the feather of Ma'at to determine the soul's worthiness.

Psychological Layer - Archetypal

Represents the ethical function of consciousness, the inner judge that weighs right against wrong. It symbolizes the development of moral reasoning and the capacity for fair judgment.

Esoteric Layer - Mystical

In Hermetic law, the scales represent cosmic justice - the principle that all actions create equal and opposite reactions. They symbolize the need for perfect equilibrium in magical work and spiritual development.

Personal Layer - Contemplative

Contemplate the scales when facing difficult decisions or seeking to understand karmic patterns. They help develop discernment and align personal will with universal justice.

Earth Element

Surface Layer - Historical

One of the four classical elements, earth represents solidity, stability, and material manifestation. In alchemical symbolism, it is depicted as a downward-pointing triangle bisected by a horizontal line.

Psychological Layer - Archetypal

Earth represents the grounding function of consciousness, practical wisdom, and connection to physical reality. It embodies patience, endurance, and the capacity to manifest ideas in tangible form.

Esoteric Layer - Mystical

In Hermetic philosophy, earth is the final stage of manifestation, where divine ideas become material reality. It represents the body of the cosmic being and the sacred vessel through which spirit expresses itself.

Personal Layer - Contemplative

Work with earth energy for grounding, stability, and bringing projects to completion. It helps establish strong foundations and maintain connection to practical wisdom.
